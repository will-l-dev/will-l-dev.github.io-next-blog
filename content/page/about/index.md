## Somehow at Next

My name is Will. I'm a Senior Platform Engineer at [Beyond](https://www.bynd.com/). This blog will document my trip to Las Vegas for the Google Cloud Next conference 2026.

The "somehow" is doing real work in that title.

---

## The long way round

I first heard about Google Next in 2021. I was a Recruiter — later Talent Acquisition Lead for one of the companies that have since been merged into Beyond, a Google Cloud Premier Partner. I was hiring engineers so we could scale, continue to build cool things, talk about Cloud. I was not, at that point, an engineer, or so I thought.

In early 2022, I began being loosely mentored by a few legendary colleagues on a roadmap to get to where I wanted to be (Ferris, Alex, Steve and Will). This is where I picked up Linux, Networking and some basic hands on Google Cloud via [cloudskillsboost.google.com](https://partner.cloudskillsboost.google.com/). Later in 2022, I took advantage of a [Government initiative](https://www.gov.uk/guidance/skills-bootcamps) to do a fully funded Software Engineering bootcamp on top of full time employment. This was before AI was a thing, and so I was able to pick up Python and to a very lesser extent C (C from the [CS50 Harvard Course](https://cs50.harvard.edu/)). Throughout all of this - getting a few Google Cloud foundation, associate and professional level certifications.

The reasons are my own, but my journey was NOT a clear plan. More because I'd been adjacent to this world for so long, even before doing a non-technical degree that I needed to know and prove to myself that I could actually do it, not just talk about it.

Turns out I could. After a pre-merger company restructure decimated us in early 2023. I had a lot of free time which I fully utilized to ramp up my upskilling. I was given an unofficial secondment opportunity into Platform Engineering while I was still technically (pun) doing Talent Acquisition. By early 2024 I had fully crossed over. In March 2026 I was promoted to Senior Platform Engineer - in my view an extremely prestigious title.

I am now, somehow, going to Google Next. But not to recruit Engineers as I had originally envisioned myself instead enjoying all the delights and free Swag as a full fledged Engineer!
